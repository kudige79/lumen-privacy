Getting started

  • Open Lumen. It starts on the Drop Zone tab.
  • Drag in a folder to process everything inside it, or drop individual files to rename just those. You can also use Choose Folder or Choose Files.
  • Click Process Files, then review the proposed names on the Results tab.
  • Approve the names you want, edit any you wish to change, then click Execute Renames. Nothing is renamed without your approval.
  • Changed your mind? The Revert Renames tab undoes any previous batch.

A full User Guide is built into the app under the Help & Privacy tab.

Common questions

Is my data private?

By default, Lumen processes your files entirely on your Mac using an on-device model, and nothing leaves your device. Cloud providers are optional, off by default, and used only with your own API key after a one-time consent prompt. Full details are in the Privacy Policy.

The on-device model will not download.

The on-device model requires an Apple silicon Mac and a one-time download of several gigabytes over the internet. Open Settings → AI Provider and use the Download control. If you are on an Intel Mac, use one of the optional cloud providers with your own API key instead.

macOS asked for permission to a folder when I renamed files.

Lumen is sandboxed and only ever touches files and folders you select. Renaming a file in place needs write access to its parent folder, so macOS presents a standard folder-access panel at the moment you execute renames. This is expected, and you grant it once per folder.

A Pages, Numbers, or Keynote file did not name well.

These files are named from their first-page preview, so they name well only when page one carries identifying text. Otherwise they are routed to the Unprocessed tab for quick manual naming rather than being renamed incorrectly.

See also the Lumen Privacy Policy.
